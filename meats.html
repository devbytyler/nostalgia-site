The Meats

Carne (Beef)

Beef is the staple meat of the Argentine asado. It is common to see cuts such as costilla (ribs), matambre (close to the ribs), falda (sides), puchero (rear), and corazon (heart.)

Pollo (Chicken)

Since beef is often expensive, it is often more economicaland just as delicious to grill up some chicken. This can be done whole chickens at a time or also just legs and thighs and other pieces. Served best with mustard and mayonnaise.

Chorizo (Sausage)

A must have at any asado. These sausages are the perfect companions to any meat and taste even better between to pieces of bread. Cheap and delicious and filled with flavor.

Morcilla (Blood Sausage)

Not for the queasy of stomach, blood sausage appeals to those true Argentines. With a very strong and salty flavor with a unique texture and color, this is the perfect meat to give an asado a little but more variety.

Chinchulin and Tripa Gorda (Intestines)

Chewy and flavorful, these bits and pieces of the intestine are also welcome to the asado, as long as you don't think to much about what you are eating.
